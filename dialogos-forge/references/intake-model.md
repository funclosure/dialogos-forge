# Intake Model

Dialogos Forge should respect how curiosity actually arrives.

Do not assume every learning thread starts as a clean topic. Many begin as fragments, long-form media, a passing question, or an attraction to a person, field, or practice.

## Core principle

Treat incoming material as an **intake mode** before treating it as a finished topic.

This lets the system route the material appropriately instead of forcing every input into the same pattern.

## Intake modes

### 1. source-digestion
Use for long-form or semi-structured media that needs extraction before deeper work begins.

Examples:
- YouTube videos
- podcasts
- interviews
- talks
- transcripts
- long essays with many possible threads

Goal:
- identify the real catalysts
- extract tensions, claims, and recurring motifs
- decide whether this becomes a topic thread, figure thread, concept thread, or is dropped

### 2. co-reading
Use for structured texts that should be read with the user rather than merely summarized.

Examples:
- PDFs
- research papers
- book chapters
- essays
- dense primary texts

Goal:
- trace arguments
- stabilize terminology
- mark confusions
- annotate passages
- connect the text to the user's broader map

### 3. skill-acquisition
Use for practical capability building.

Examples:
- React Native
- databases
- frameworks
- toolchains
- engineering patterns

Goal:
- move from vague desire to concrete skill path
- identify prerequisites
- produce exercises, projects, and embodiment steps

### 4. figure-inquiry
Use when the user wants to understand what a person represents, not just their biography or summary.

Examples:
- philosophers
- scientists
- designers
- public intellectuals
- key builders or theorists

Goal:
- understand the figure's central stance
- identify their main distinctions and contributions
- place them in a lineage or map of influence
- clarify why they matter

### 5. concept-inquiry
Use for abstract ideas that need sustained clarification.

Examples:
- grounding
- selfhood
- agency
- intelligence
- relevance realization

Goal:
- clarify meanings
- compare rival framings
- refine distinctions
- connect the concept to lived and technical contexts

### 6. embodiment-inquiry
Use for body-based or organism-level learning.

Examples:
- fascia
- movement practices
- Animal Flow
- energy
- posture
- somatic frameworks

Goal:
- understand the concept or practice
- relate it to felt experience
- avoid reducing embodiment to disembodied explanation

## Routing questions

When a new input arrives, ask implicitly or explicitly:

1. What kind of thing is this?
2. Does it need digestion, co-reading, practice, or conceptual inquiry?
3. Is the likely destination a skill path, a figure map, a concept thread, or a transient note?
4. Is there enough heat here to open a state file yet?

## Typical destinations

- `source-digestion` often starts in `dialogos/inbox/` and may branch into figure, concept, or synthesis work
- `co-reading` usually starts in `dialogos/inbox/` or `dialogos/forge/`, with a linked topic state file if the text becomes central
- `skill-acquisition` usually creates both a topic artifact and a state file quickly
- `figure-inquiry` often produces a stable synthesis once the figure's shape becomes clear
- `concept-inquiry` often cycles through forge and state repeatedly before synthesis
- `embodiment-inquiry` should preserve both conceptual notes and practical embodiment paths

## Rule of fit

Do not force the user into a rigid curriculum.

Many learners, especially nonlinear ones, move by salience first and structure second. Dialogos Forge should support magnetic attraction, later clarification, and then durable synthesis.
