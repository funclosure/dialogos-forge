## Why It Has Heat
This feels central because it is not just a talk about interface design. It is trying to name a civilizational bottleneck: modern knowledge work has trapped thought inside tiny symbolic rectangles, and a more humane medium would let humans think with more of their body, senses, and spatial intelligence.

It also feels adjacent to several existing concerns: embodiment, dynamic representations, explorable models, philosophy of mind, software authoring, and the relationship between medium and thought.

### Initial pulls
- Representations enable thoughts that were previously unthinkable.
- Great ideas often ride on great representations.
- Print and screen-based knowledge work empowered some capabilities while atrophying others.
- Tiny-rectangle knowledge work is described as inhumane because it restricts the full range of human intellectual capacities.
- The dynamic medium is framed as the next medium of thought after print.
- A humane medium should be computational, responsive, connected, physical, and body-scale.
- Knowledge objects should become explorable models, not piles of words.
- Programming today conflates engineering and authoring.
- Humane design will not happen by default; it requires deliberate value-laden design.
